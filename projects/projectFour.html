Client

IADB

When?

2020

The project

The Inter-American Development Bank is an international financial institution headquartered in Washington, D.C., United States of America, and serving as the largest source of development financing for Latin America and the Caribbean.

I was contacted by them when the COVID crisis started to design a News Room so they could inform the public about the COVID situation.

We had a week to get this done.

The project was a success and they loved how it turned out, so they contacted me again to create a new COVID platform and re-design their main landing page.

The team

I worked closely with their content people and BE developers, but had to take care of the design and FE work by myself.

Goals

  • Create a News Room where all news related to COVID could be posted daily.

  • Create a COVID platform where the IADB could share resources COVID related.

  • Redesign the landing page using their corporate identity manual.

Users

  • The main type of user for this project were journalists from all over the world.

  • This site is also visited by politicians and public in general from every country.

Process

The communications team from the IADB contacted me, explained the project and the rush they had to get this online.

I designed three different high-fi versions for the News Room and sent them for feedback and approval.

We made a few iterations on the design and started working on the FE once the design was closed.

Once the News Room was online, they contacted me again to re-design their landing page, they wanted a "fresher" design, but keeping it alligned to their identity manual. We had more time, so I worked on the layout with low-fi designs and then made several iterations until reaching a final product that the client was happy with. I worked on the FE as well.

While we were working on the landing page, they also explained that the News Room had been a success, and they wanted to create a hub where they could post COVID resources. So we worked on it simultaneously.

Once the project was done, I had a few meetings with their design team to teach them how to work images so they could maintain the site after my work was done.
